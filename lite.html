💤 Hibern8 Ctrl+Space Lite — Help

Also known as “HiberSpace” — a play on “hyperspace” and shorthand for Hibern8 Ctrl+Space Lite.

🔒 No information leaves your device through Hibern8 Ctrl+Space Lite. It has no servers and sends nothing about you, your tabs, or your browsing anywhere. Everything runs locally in your browser profile; the only things it saves on your device are your display preferences (theme and accent color) and the two Safe-Hold toggles.

What it does

  • Hibernate tabs & windows — per-tab 💤; "Hibernate idle tabs" sweeps every window; "💤 Window" on an active tab frees just that window. Active, pinned, and audio/video tabs are always spared.
  • See audio/video tabs — anything playing sound is listed at the top, with Go and Mute.
  • See active tabs — the foreground tab of each window, with Go and a one-click window hibernate.
  • Memory & power estimates — the metrics row shows hibernated/awake counts and the approximate MB reclaimed and watts saved.

Prove the savings

Open your browser's task manager — Shift+Esc in Chrome/Edge, or about:performance in Firefox — and watch hibernated tabs' memory collapse. The in-app numbers are illustrative estimates; the task manager shows the truth.

Safe-Hold (public-network privacy)

🛡 Suspend holds all page traffic, pauses any in-progress downloads (resuming on release), and keeps tabs from loading until you release it — so nothing reloads or keeps transferring on untrusted Wi‑Fi before your VPN is up. Release is always manual.

A browser extension can't actually detect a VPN, so this is a manual confirmation — releasing asks you to confirm your VPN is up. The strongest protection is your VPN client's own kill-switch; this is a convenience layer on top of it.

Privacy & security

No information leaves your device through Hibern8 Ctrl+Space Lite — no servers, analytics, ads, or third-party code, and no data collected or transmitted. Everything is local to your browser profile. A strict Content-Security-Policy blocks any inline or remote script, and an injection watchdog warns you in-panel (and prompts an antivirus scan) if one is ever blocked.
